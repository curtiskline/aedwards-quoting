Chip,

This is a good list. You are right about the standard: the tool isn't complete until your people are actually using it. I'll propose changes here to work towards your goal, making a TODO list from your email.

Opening the files customers send: fixed, no charge. The file a customer sends is often the whole job. As of this week, it saves with the quote and opens right from the quote page. Nobody has to go digging through email to find it.

Deleting bad quotes: already promised, still free. I'll add it this week.

Quoting the same job to several bidders: I'll make your workaround a button. Changing the name by hand works, but you shouldn't have to. I'm adding a "Duplicate quote" button. Copy the quote, pick the other customer, done. No charge.

Oddball items and comments: already in the tool. Any line on a quote can be typed in free-hand with its own description and price, and there's a comment line for notes that don't carry a price. I'll send you a short screen recording this week that shows both in about a minute.

Adding new products: you can do part of this yourself today. Product types you can already add in the admin screen (the recording will show that too). Adding new catalog items with pricing still needs me. Fixing it is one of the three real additions below.

That leaves the three items that are genuinely new work. Since you asked me to speak up on cost, here is the answer:

  1. Quotes go out from your people, not a robot address. Nothing has ever gone out without a person reading it and clicking send, but the "from" line should be that person, and the customer's reply should land in their real inbox. I'll make it so. It needs one permission step in your Office 365, which I'll coordinate with Jackson Technical so it's no work for your folks.
  2. Revising a quote when the scope changes. When a customer changes the job, your people hit "Revise," get a new version linked to the original, and the old one closes out as replaced. One project, full history, nothing left hanging open.
  3. A catalog your team owns end to end. Add products and pricing yourselves, no developer in the loop.

All three together: $3,000 fixed, done in about two weeks. Payment works the way you proposed: 25% down to start the work, and I'll invoice the balance at the start as well, on your usual 45 days. Bug fixes and small stuff stay free, like always. That number is only for the new work above, and it doesn't move once we shake on it.

Last, on eventually moving to a more automated system: agreed. Get the tool solid in your people's daily hands first, then take automation one step at a time while customers get used to it.

Devin
